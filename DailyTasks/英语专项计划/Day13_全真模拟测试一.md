**Passage 1: Healthy Eating Habits**

Good eating habits are important for our health. Here are some tips to help you eat healthily.

First, eat breakfast every day. Breakfast is the most important meal. It gives you energy for the morning. Try to eat whole grains, fruits, and protein for breakfast.

Second, eat more fruits and vegetables. They contain vitamins and minerals that your body needs. Try to eat at least five servings of fruits and vegetables every day. Choose different colors to get different nutrients.

Third, limit junk food. Foods like chips, candy, and soda have a lot of sugar and fat but few nutrients. They can lead to health problems like obesity and heart disease. It's OK to eat them sometimes, but not every day.

Fourth, drink plenty of water. Water helps your body work properly. Try to drink 6-8 glasses of water every day. Avoid sugary drinks like soda and juice.

Finally, don't skip meals. Eating regular meals helps keep your energy level stable and prevents overeating later. Try to eat three meals and one or two healthy snacksæ¯å¤©.

**Passage 2: The Power of Kindness**

When I was in Grade 7, I had a classmate named Sarah. She was new to our school and didn't have many friends. She was quiet and shy, and some students made fun of her because she wore old clothes and had an unusual accent.

**Passage 3: Electric Cars**

Electric cars are becoming more popular around the world. Unlike traditional cars that run on gasoline, electric cars use batteries and electric motors. This makes them better for the environment and cheaper to operate.

One of the main advantages of electric cars is that they produce zero emissions. This means they don't release harmful gases into the air like gasoline cars do. This is important for fighting air pollution and climate change.

Electric cars are also cheaper to run. Electricity costs less than gasoline, and electric cars require less maintenance. They don't need oil changes, and their brakes last longer because of regenerative braking, which captures energy when you slow down.

However, electric cars also have some disadvantages. The main problem is their limited range. Most electric cars can only travel 200-300 kilometers on a single charge. This is less than gasoline cars. Also, charging takes much longer than filling up with gas. It can take several hours to fully charge an electric car.

Another issue is the lack of charging stations. While there are gas stations everywhere, charging stations for electric cars are still not common in many areas. This "range anxiety" makes some people hesitant to buy electric cars.

Despite these challenges, electric car technology is improving rapidly. Batteries are getting better, allowing cars to travel farther. More charging stations are being built. Many countries are planning to ban gasoline cars in the future. It seems clear that electric cars will play an important role in transportation in the coming years.
